Foundational Readings

Key texts for researchers entering or deepening their work in music psychology and neuroscience:

  • Peretz, I., & Coltheart, M. (2003). Modularity of music processing. Nature Neuroscience, 6(7), 688–691. — A foundational paper on the modular organisation of music perception in the brain.
  • Huron, D. (2006). Sweet Anticipation: Music and the Psychology of Expectation. MIT Press. — Comprehensive theory of musical expectation grounded in evolutionary psychology.
  • Zatorre, R. J., & Salimpoor, V. N. (2013). From perception to pleasure: Music and its neural substrates. Science, 340(6129), 1231–1235. — Overview of the neuroscience of musical reward and emotion.
  • Levitin, D. J. (2006). This Is Your Brain on Music. Dutton. — An accessible introduction to the cognitive neuroscience of music for broad audiences.
  • Deutsch, D. (Ed.). (2013). The Psychology of Music (3rd ed.). Academic Press. — Comprehensive handbook covering major topics in music psychology.
Key Journals
  • Music Perception (University of California Press) — Leading peer-reviewed journal covering all aspects of music psychology and neuroscience.
  • Psychomusicology: Music, Mind, and Brain (APA) — Publishes research on the psychological and neural foundations of music.
  • Frontiers in Psychology: Auditory Cognitive Neuroscience (Frontiers Media) — Open-access journal covering auditory and music cognition.
  • Annals of the New York Academy of Sciences — Regularly features thematic volumes on music and the brain, including the Neurosciences and Music series.
  • Journal of New Music Research (Taylor & Francis) — Interdisciplinary journal spanning musicology, music technology, and music cognition.
Open Datasets & Tools

EEG & Neuroimaging:

  • EEGLAB — Open-source MATLAB toolbox for EEG analysis (Swartz Center for Computational Neuroscience).
  • MNE-Python — Python-based package for processing EEG, MEG, and fNIRS data.
  • OpenNeuro — Platform for sharing and discovering neuroimaging datasets, including music cognition studies.

Behavioural & Music Analysis:

  • PsychoPy — Open-source software for designing and running psychology experiments.
  • music21 — Python toolkit for computational musicology and music analysis.
  • Essentia — Open-source C++/Python library for audio analysis and music information retrieval (UPF).
  • MIDI Toolbox — MATLAB toolbox for analysing MIDI files in music research contexts.
