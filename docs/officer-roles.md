# APSMMB Officer Roles and Responsibilities

The Executive Committee of APSMMB consists of eight elected officers. All officers serve two-year terms and are expected to dedicate approximately 2–5 hours per week to their duties.

---

## President

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Provide strategic leadership and vision for the Society.
- Preside over all Executive Committee and General meetings.
- Serve as the primary spokesperson and representative of APSMMB to external organisations and the public.
- Coordinate the work of the Executive Committee and ensure accountability to the membership.
- Sign official documents and correspondence on behalf of the Society.
- Liaise with partner societies and institutions.

**Qualifications:** Full Member with prior experience in academic leadership or scientific society management preferred.

---

## Vice President

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Support the President in all duties and assume Presidential responsibilities in the President's absence.
- Lead specific strategic initiatives or working groups as assigned by the President.
- Oversee succession planning and officer onboarding.
- Manage relationships with institutional partners and sponsors.

**Qualifications:** Full Member in good standing.

---

## Secretary General

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Manage official correspondence of the Society.
- Record and distribute minutes of all Executive Committee and General meetings (see `meeting-minutes-template.md`).
- Maintain the Society's official records, governance documents, and archives.
- Coordinate communications between Executive Committee members.
- Manage meeting scheduling and logistics.
- Maintain and update the repository of official society documents.

**Qualifications:** Full Member with strong organisational and communication skills.

---

## Treasurer

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Manage the Society's financial accounts and transactions.
- Prepare and present annual financial reports to the membership at the AGM.
- Oversee membership dues collection in coordination with the Membership Officer.
- Manage budgets for events and projects in coordination with relevant officers.
- Ensure compliance with any applicable financial regulations.
- Advise the Executive Committee on financial matters.

**Qualifications:** Full Member; financial management or accounting experience preferred.

---

## Membership Officer

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Process and review membership applications.
- Maintain the membership database and directory.
- Communicate with prospective and current members regarding applications and renewals.
- Coordinate membership drives and promotional campaigns.
- Prepare membership reports for the Executive Committee.
- Facilitate the mentorship programme pairing experienced members with early-career researchers.

**Qualifications:** Full Member with good interpersonal and administrative skills.

---

## Events Officer

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Plan, coordinate, and oversee APSMMB events, including the Annual Symposium, seminar series, and workshops.
- Issue calls for event proposals and evaluate submissions.
- Manage relationships with local organising committees and venue partners.
- Coordinate event registration and logistics.
- Ensure events are accessible, inclusive, and aligned with the Society's values.
- Report on event outcomes to the Executive Committee.

**Qualifications:** Full Member with experience in academic event organisation preferred.

---

## Website & Communications Officer

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Maintain and update the APSMMB website (this repository).
- Manage the Society's social media accounts and digital presence.
- Draft and distribute the Society's newsletter and announcements.
- Coordinate with other officers to ensure timely and accurate communications.
- Manage the GitHub repository and review pull requests from contributors.
- Ensure the website remains accessible, up-to-date, and consistent with the Society's visual identity.

**Qualifications:** Full Member with familiarity in web technologies (HTML/CSS) and digital communications.

---

## Ethics Officer

**Primary Responsibilities:**
- Receive and handle reports of Code of Conduct violations, ensuring confidentiality.
- Advise the Executive Committee on ethical matters and policy.
- Lead the review and updating of the Code of Conduct as needed.
- Coordinate investigations and enforcement proceedings fairly and impartially.
- Maintain records of ethics reports and outcomes (anonymised).
- Serve as a resource for members with ethical concerns.

**Qualifications:** Full Member with demonstrated commitment to inclusive and ethical research practice; legal or ethics training is an asset.
