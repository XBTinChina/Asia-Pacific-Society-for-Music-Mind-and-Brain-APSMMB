# APSMMB Bylaws

## Article I — Name and Purpose

**Section 1.1 — Name**
The name of this organization is the Asia-Pacific Society for Music, Mind, and Brain, hereinafter referred to as "APSMMB" or "the Society."

**Section 1.2 — Purpose**
The Society is organized exclusively for scientific and educational purposes, including:
- Fostering research in music psychology, music neuroscience, and related disciplines across the Asia-Pacific region.
- Organizing and supporting academic conferences, seminars, and workshops.
- Facilitating networking and professional development among members.
- Maintaining a digital presence and open-access resource portal for the benefit of the research community.
- Promoting culturally diverse and inclusive approaches to the scientific study of music.

**Section 1.3 — Non-Profit Status**
APSMMB operates on a not-for-profit basis. No part of the Society's earnings shall benefit any individual member or officer.

## Article II — Membership

**Section 2.1 — Categories of Membership**

| Category | Eligibility | Annual Dues | Voting Rights |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Full Member | Researchers, clinicians, or practitioners with an active interest in music and mind/brain sciences | [TBC] USD | Yes |
| Student Member | Currently enrolled graduate or undergraduate students | [TBC] USD (reduced) | Yes (advisory) |
| Affiliate Member | Professionals from adjacent fields with interest in music science | [TBC] USD | No |
| Honorary Member | Individuals of distinguished achievement elected by the Executive Committee | Waived | Honorary |

**Section 2.2 — Application**
Individuals seeking membership shall submit an application via the Society's website or designated form, including a brief statement of research interests and agreement to the Code of Conduct.

**Section 2.3 — Dues**
Annual membership dues shall be set by the Executive Committee and reviewed biennially. Dues shall be payable upon joining and upon annual renewal.

**Section 2.4 — Termination of Membership**
Membership may be terminated for non-payment of dues after a 60-day grace period, or for violations of the Code of Conduct following due process.

## Article III — Governance

**Section 3.1 — Executive Committee**
The Society shall be governed by an Executive Committee consisting of the following elected officers:
1. President
2. Vice President
3. Secretary General
4. Treasurer
5. Membership Officer
6. Events Officer
7. Website & Communications Officer
8. Ethics Officer

**Section 3.2 — Terms of Office**
Officers shall serve two-year terms, renewable once. Terms shall be staggered to ensure continuity.

**Section 3.3 — Duties**
Duties of each officer are described in the Officer Roles document (see `docs/officer-roles.md`).

**Section 3.4 — Vacancies**
In the event of a vacancy, the Executive Committee may appoint an interim officer to serve until the next scheduled election.

---

## Article IV — Meetings

**Section 4.1 — Annual General Meeting**
The Society shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) at least once per calendar year, which may be held virtually. All members shall be given at least 30 days' notice.

**Section 4.2 — Executive Committee Meetings**
The Executive Committee shall meet no less than quarterly. Meetings may be held in person or via video conference.

**Section 4.3 — Quorum**
A quorum for Executive Committee meetings shall consist of five (5) of the eight (8) officers. A quorum for general membership votes shall consist of 20% of Full Members.

**Section 4.4 — Minutes**
Minutes shall be recorded at all Executive Committee meetings and made available to members upon request. See `docs/meeting-minutes-template.md`.

---

## Article V — Elections

**Section 5.1 — Eligibility**
Candidates for officer positions must be Full Members in good standing.

**Section 5.2 — Nominations**
A Nominations Committee, appointed by the President, shall solicit nominations at least 60 days prior to the election.

**Section 5.3 — Voting**
Elections shall be conducted by secret ballot. All Full Members in good standing are eligible to vote. Elections shall be decided by simple majority.

**Section 5.4 — Inaugural Election**
The inaugural Executive Committee shall be constituted through a nomination and appointment process overseen by the founding members.

---

## Article VI — Amendments

**Section 6.1 — Proposal**
Amendments to these bylaws may be proposed by any Full Member or by the Executive Committee in writing.

**Section 6.2 — Approval**
Amendments require approval by two-thirds (2/3) of voting Full Members, with at least 30 days' notice given before the vote.

**Section 6.3 — Effective Date**
Amendments take effect immediately upon approval unless otherwise specified.

---

## Article VII — Dissolution

In the event of dissolution, after payment of all obligations, remaining assets shall be transferred to one or more organizations serving similar scientific or educational purposes, as determined by the Executive Committee.
