About APSMMB

Our story, mission, and the people behind the society

Our Story

The Asia-Pacific Society for Music, Mind, and Brain was founded in 2025 in response to a recognised gap in the global landscape of music science. While excellent societies exist in Europe and North America — such as the Society for Music Perception and Cognition (SMPC) and the European Society for the Cognitive Sciences of Music (ESCOM) — researchers across the vast and culturally rich Asia-Pacific region lacked a dedicated organisational home. The founding members of APSMMB sought to change this, bringing together colleagues from East Asia, Southeast Asia, South Asia, Australia, New Zealand, and the Pacific Islands under a shared commitment to advancing the scientific study of music.

APSMMB was conceived not simply as a regional chapter of existing organisations, but as a society with a distinct identity and purpose: to conduct rigorous, internationally competitive research that is simultaneously grounded in the cultural realities of the Asia-Pacific. The region's extraordinary diversity of musical traditions — from the gamelan orchestras of Indonesia to the rāga traditions of South Asia, from Cantopop to Australian First Nations music — offers an unparalleled natural laboratory for studying the relationships between music, culture, mind, and brain. APSMMB aspires to leverage this unique advantage in inaugurating a new era of music science that is globally rigorous and regionally distinctive.

Mission & Scope

"To foster a collaborative and culturally diverse research community by organizing academic seminars and conferences, maintaining an active digital presence, and facilitating member networking and professional development. Rooted in the rich cultural landscape of the Asia-Pacific, APSMMB aspires to inaugurate a new era of music science that is both globally rigorous and regionally distinctive."

Areas of Scope

APSMMB encompasses research across a broad range of disciplines at the intersection of music, mind, and brain:

Music Psychology Music Neuroscience Music Therapy Ethnomusicology Computational Musicology Music & Language Music Perception & Cognition Music & Emotion Developmental Music Cognition Cross-Cultural Music Research

Strategic Goals (2025–2027)

In our first three years, APSMMB is committed to achieving the following strategic goals:

  1. Establish a Regional Member Network
    Build an active membership base spanning researchers, clinicians, and practitioners from across the Asia-Pacific, with particular outreach to underrepresented nations and institutions.
  2. Launch an Annual Symposium
    Organise the inaugural APSMMB Symposium in 2025, establishing a flagship event for the presentation and discussion of cutting-edge research in music, mind, and brain.
  3. Create a Curated Open-Access Resource Portal
    Develop and maintain a publicly accessible repository of foundational readings, research tools, datasets, journals, and funding opportunities relevant to music scientists in the Asia-Pacific.
  4. Support Early-Career Researchers
    Implement dedicated programmes for graduate students and early-career researchers, including mentorship pairings, travel grants, and dedicated conference sessions.
  5. Foster Cross-Cultural and Interdisciplinary Collaboration
    Actively promote and support research projects that cross national, cultural, and disciplinary boundaries, reflecting the unique strengths and diversity of the Asia-Pacific region.
  6. Partner with International Societies
    Establish formal partnerships with SMPC, ESCOM, APSCOM, and other leading organisations to ensure APSMMB members benefit from a connected global network.
